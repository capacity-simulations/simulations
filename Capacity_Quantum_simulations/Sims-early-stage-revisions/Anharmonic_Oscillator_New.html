Quantum Wave Packet vs Classical Particle

i
This simulation compares the behavior of a classical particle (pink ball) with a quantum wave packet (blue distribution) in a potential well V(x) = x² + εx⁴.

When ε = 0, the potential is purely harmonic (simple parabola), and both classical and quantum systems oscillate similarly. As ε increases, the potential becomes anharmonic, causing the quantum wave packet to spread and eventually split into multiple peaks (cat states) due to different energy components evolving at different rates.

The classical particle always follows a definite trajectory, while the quantum probability distribution shows the inherent uncertainty in quantum mechanics.
