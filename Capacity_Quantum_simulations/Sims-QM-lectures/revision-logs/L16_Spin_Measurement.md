EC 04/05/26: The spin-measurement simulation was revised to be more lecture-friendly and less visually cluttered. The right-side panel was streamlined by reducing typography weight, flattening the measurement controls, simplifying the probability display so `P(↑)` and `P(↓)` read as informational bars rather than buttons, and toning down the custom-state and history styling. The old `Undo Measurement` and `Reset to |↑⟩` panel buttons were removed, and the title bar was extended across the full layout with a single header-level `Reset` control so global actions live in one consistent place. A new `Visuals` section with a default-on `Lecture display` checkbox was added to hide the `Set Custom State` and `Measurement History` sections during presentations. Follow-up refinements changed both probability bars to blue shading, made X/Y history outcomes read `Spin +` / `Spin -` instead of `Spin Up` / `Spin Down`, restored lowercase rendering for the Greek `α` and `β` labels, and changed the state-vector line pointing to the white marker so it is drawn white for clearer visual emphasis. These changes were made to improve scanability, reduce student-facing distraction, and produce a cleaner projection layout without altering the underlying measurement physics.

EC 04/09/26: The Bloch-sphere reference planes were adjusted to reduce visual confusion with the probability-colored surface. The earlier filled colored great-circle planes were removed because they read like part of the probability map, then reintroduced only as thin white outline circles on the `xy`, `xz`, and `yz` planes so viewers can still judge where the axes intersect the sphere without adding competing color fills.
