EC 04/02/26: The harmonic oscillator simulation was updated so the user now sets \(m\) and \(\omega\) directly, with the wavefunction width, energy spacing, turning points, and potential all recalculated consistently from the full harmonic-oscillator formulas rather than a spring-constant-only shortcut. The potential panel was simplified by removing the decorative state overlays, and the UI was cleaned up by replacing the old override-\(\omega\) control with a display-only animation-speed slider and removing the extra stats/time readouts. These changes were made to keep the simulation closer to the lecture notation and to make the plots more physically honest and easier to interpret.

EC 04/09/26: The `Lecture display` mode in `L07_harmonic-oscillator.html` was updated to behave as a cleaner teaching view and is now configured that way by default. When lecture display is checked, the simulation opens paused, the wavefunction panel shows only the real part at the phase where \(\operatorname{Im}\psi = 0\), the imaginary-part trace and legend entry are hidden, and the animation-speed slider is hidden. The play and reset behavior were also adjusted so lecture mode stays on that paused real-part snapshot unless lecture display is turned off. These changes were made to match the lecture-facing presentation used elsewhere in the Lecture 7 simulations while preserving the full animated complex view outside lecture mode.
