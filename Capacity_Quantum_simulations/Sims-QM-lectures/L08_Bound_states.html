Quantum Finite Square Well - Bound States Simulation
About the Simulation

This simulation visualizes bound states in a finite quantum potential well, demonstrating how discrete energy levels emerge from mathematical constraints. The potential matches the lecture notes: V = 0 outside the well and V = −V₀ for |x| < a; bound-state energies E are quoted with zero at infinity (so E < 0).

The constant ℏ²/(2m) is set to a pedagogical value (not the SI free-electron value) so that typical well depths and widths produce a clear number of bound states in this demo.

Bound-state energies are fixed by the intersection of a transcendental curve with a circle in the (k, η) plane:

Here k is the wave number inside the well, η is related to decay outside the well, and a is the half-width. Each intersection corresponds to an allowed energy.

N is the number of bound states for the currently selected parity (even or odd). Adjust V₀ to see states appear/disappear as the circle expands/contracts. Finite wells support only a finite number of bound states.
