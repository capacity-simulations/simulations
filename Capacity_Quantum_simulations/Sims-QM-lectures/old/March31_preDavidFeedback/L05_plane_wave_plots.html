About this simulation

A free quantum particle with \(V(x)=0\) in natural units \(\hbar=1\), \(2m=1\). The eigenstates are plane waves \(\psi(x)=e^{ikx}\) labelled by the real wavenumber \(k\).

Physics

The time-independent Schrödinger equation reduces to \(-\tfrac{\hbar^2}{2m}\psi''=E\psi\) with solutions \(\psi(x)=e^{ikx}\) for \(k\in\mathbb{R}\) and energy \(E=\hbar^2 k^2/2m\). The momentum is \(p=\hbar k\) and the de Broglie wavelength is \(\lambda=2\pi/|k|\). These states are not normalisable on \((-\infty,+\infty)\).

\[E = \frac{\hbar^2 k^2}{2m},\quad \psi(x)=e^{ikx},\quad \lambda=\frac{2\pi}{|k|},\quad p=\hbar k\]
Visuals

Dispersion relation: \(E(k)=k^2\) parabola with the current \(k\) highlighted.
Wavefunction: real (blue) and imaginary (green) parts of \(e^{ikx}\) over a finite window; optional constant \(|\psi|^2=1\) band (yellow).

Controls

Wavenumber: slider for \(k\). Display: toggles for \(|\psi|^2\) and time evolution. Animation: time speed slider. Current State: \(k\), \(E\), \(\lambda\), \(p\), and lecture equations. Top bar: Reset / Pause.
