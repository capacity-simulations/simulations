About This Simulation

Spherical harmonics Yℓm(θ, φ) are special mathematical functions defined on the surface of a sphere. They arise naturally as solutions to the angular part of Laplace's equation and play a fundamental role in quantum mechanics — describing the angular distribution of electron orbitals, atomic structure, and angular momentum eigenstates.

The quantum number ℓ (degree) controls the total number of nodal lines, while m (order) determines their orientation. Toggle between real and complex representations, and visualize using a point cloud (probability-weighted sampling) or polar surface (radial distance encodes |Y|).
