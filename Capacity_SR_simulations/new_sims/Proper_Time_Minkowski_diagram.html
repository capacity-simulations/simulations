About This Simulation

This interactive Minkowski diagram demonstrates the concept of proper time in Einstein's Special Relativity. Proper time (τ) is the time measured by a clock traveling along a specific worldline between two spacetime events.

The simulation illustrates the Twin Paradox: among all timelike worldlines connecting two events, the straight (inertial) path always yields the maximum proper time.

Reversed Triangle Inequality

In Minkowski geometry, the straight inertial worldline maximizes proper time:

τstraight ≥ τbent ≥ τzigzag

E₁ is pinned at the origin and its future light cone (shaded) is fixed. Drag E₂ and the turning points P, P₁, P₂ within the timelike region (strictly inside the light cone, not on the null rays). Paths are kept future-directed and timelike automatically.

Each worldline's proper time is computed using the Minkowski metric:

Δτ = √(Δt² − Δx²)  (c = 1)

Drag the events and turning points to explore how different paths through spacetime result in different elapsed proper times.
