About This Simulation

This interactive Minkowski diagram demonstrates the concept of proper time in Einstein's Special Relativity. Proper time (τ) is the time measured by a clock traveling along a specific worldline between two spacetime events.

The simulation illustrates the Twin Paradox: among all timelike worldlines connecting two events, the straight (inertial) path always yields the maximum proper time. This is the reversed triangle inequality of Minkowski geometry:

τstraight ≥ τbent ≥ τzigzag

Each worldline's proper time is computed using the Minkowski metric:

Δτ = √(Δt² − Δx²/c²)

Drag the events and turning points to explore how different paths through spacetime result in different elapsed proper times. The animated pulses move at a rate proportional to proper time along each path.
