About This Simulation

This interactive simulation demonstrates rotational symmetry across different regular polygons. Use the shared rotation slider to rotate all shapes simultaneously and discover the angles at which each shape maps back onto itself.

A shape has rotational symmetry at angle θ if rotating it by θ around its center produces an identical configuration. The circle has continuous symmetry, while regular polygons with n sides return to their starting configuration at multiples of 360°/n.

Symmetry groups: each shape belongs to a cyclic group Cn. The circle belongs to SO(2), which contains all planar rotations.

The pink glow indicates when a shape is at a symmetric angle. Discovered angles are recorded in the control panel on the right.
