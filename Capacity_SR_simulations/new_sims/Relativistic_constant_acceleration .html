About This Simulation

This simulation demonstrates relativistic constant-force acceleration, where a particle is pushed by a constant force F. Unlike classical mechanics, special relativity imposes a universal speed limit: the speed of light c.

F = dp/dt,   p = γmv,   γ = 1/√(1 − v²/c²)

As the particle accelerates, its relativistic momentum increases without bound, but its velocity asymptotically approaches c and never exceeds it. The velocity graph shows how the relativistic velocity (blue) diverges from the classical prediction (pink), which incorrectly exceeds c.

The energy graph shows total relativistic energy E = γmc², relativistic kinetic energy K = (γ−1)mc², and classical kinetic energy ½mv². At high speeds, relativistic energy grows much faster than classical predictions.
