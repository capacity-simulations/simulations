About This Simulation

This simulation demonstrates Galilean relativity — the principle that the laws of motion are the same in all inertial (non-accelerating) reference frames.

A ball is thrown vertically upward inside a train carriage moving at velocity v relative to a platform. The throw speed is u relative to the carriage.

In the train frame (S'): The ball goes straight up and comes back down — a simple vertical trajectory with x' = 0.

In the platform frame (S): The ball has a horizontal component vₓ = v and traces a parabolic arc, since x = vt.

Switch between frames to see how the same physical event looks different to different observers. No relativistic effects are included — this is classical (Galilean) mechanics.
