Polar vs Cartesian

About This Simulation

This interactive visualization demonstrates uniform circular motion — a fundamental concept in physics where an object moves along a circular path at constant speed.

The radar screen (left) shows a blip orbiting the origin at constant radius R and constant angular speed ω. A sweeping radar arm follows the blip's angular position.

The Cartesian plot decomposes this motion into x(t) = R·cos(ωt + θ₀) and y(t) = R·sin(ωt + θ₀) — two sinusoids 90° out of phase. This reveals how circular motion is really two coupled oscillations.

The Polar plot shows the same motion in polar coordinates: r(t) is a flat horizontal line (constant radius) and θ(t) is a linear ramp (angle grows steadily). What looks complex in Cartesian coordinates becomes elegantly simple in polar form.

This contrast illustrates a key insight from physics: choosing the right coordinate system can transform a complex problem into a trivial one. The motion resets after one full period T = 2π/ω.
