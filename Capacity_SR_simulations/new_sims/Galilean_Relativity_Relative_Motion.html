About This Simulation

This interactive simulation demonstrates Galilean relativity — the principle that the laws of motion are the same in all inertial (non-accelerating) reference frames.

Two observers, A and B, move at constant velocities along a straight line. A ball moves at a chosen velocity relative to A. Switch frames (A, B, ball, or ground) to see how the same event looks different to different observers.

Velocities transform by simple addition/subtraction, but relative velocities (differences) are the same in every inertial frame.

Scene info: Top-down view of 1D motion along x. Grid spacing = 1 m. Trails are shown in the active frame. Arrows indicate velocity vectors (scaled).
