About This Simulation

This interactive Minkowski diagram demonstrates the relativity of simultaneity — a key consequence of Einstein's special relativity.

Two events that are simultaneous in one reference frame (S) are generally not simultaneous in another frame (S') moving at velocity v relative to S.

Lorentz Transform:
ct' = γ(ct − βx)
x'  = γ(x − β·ct)
where γ = 1/√(1 − β²), β = v/c

For two events simultaneous in S (same ct) separated by Δx:

Δct' = −γβΔx

This shows that if Δx ≠ 0 and β ≠ 0, the events occur at different times in S'.

How to use: Drag events A and B on the diagram (both events share the same ct in S, so they stay simultaneous in S). Adjust v/c with the slider. The dashed lines show surfaces of constant ct' through each event.
