About This Simulation

Two reference frames map the same plot of land — frame S aligns its axes to X and Y; frame S′ is rotated by an angle θ with axes X′ and Y′. Drag the trees and watch coordinate readings in S and S′ disagree wildly.

Yet the distance between any two trees stays perfectly identical in both frames.

D² = ΔX² + ΔY² (same in both frames)

Observers in different frames disagree on coordinates, but agree on invariant distances. Coordinates are labels — geometry is real.

★ The Bridge to Relativity

Here, observers disagree on (X, Y) but agree on Euclidean distance:

D² = ΔX² + ΔY²

In spacetime, observers in different inertial frames disagree on time and space coordinates, but agree on the invariant interval:

S² = −c²Δt² + ΔX² + ΔY² + ΔZ²

Coordinates are merely labels we choose. Geometry is real.
