About This Simulation

This simulation demonstrates the muon-mountain problem, one of the most compelling real-world proofs of Einstein's Special Relativity.

Muons are subatomic particles created when cosmic rays hit the upper atmosphere (~10 km altitude). They have a very short rest-frame lifetime:

τ₀ = 2.2 μs

At their speed (~0.998c), classical physics predicts they should only travel ~660 m before decaying — far short of reaching the ground.

However, due to relativistic time dilation, their lifetime is extended by the Lorentz factor γ:

τ = γ · τ₀ where γ = 1/√(1 − v²/c²)

At v = 0.998c, γ ≈ 15.8, so the dilated lifetime allows them to travel ~10,400 m — enough to reach Earth's surface. This is confirmed experimentally!

Animation motion uses a schematic progress coordinate along a fixed 10 km track. Playback speed only changes how fast that progress advances; it does not rescale physics when you change v/c. Distances, γ, and lifetimes are always computed from the formulas above.
