About This Simulation

This interactive simulator visualizes how momentum behaves differently in Einstein's Special Relativity compared to classical (Newtonian) mechanics.

In classical physics, momentum is simply p = mv. However, as an object's velocity approaches the speed of light c, relativistic effects become significant, and the true momentum diverges toward infinity.

The green curve shows the relativistic momentum, which grows without bound as v → c, while the blue line shows the classical approximation, which remains linear. The pink marker highlights the deviation between the two at your selected velocity.

p = γm₀v  where  γ = 1 / √(1 − v²/c²)
As v → c, γ → ∞, so p → ∞
