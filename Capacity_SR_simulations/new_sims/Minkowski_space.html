About This Simulation

This interactive visualizer demonstrates Minkowski spacetime — the geometric framework of Einstein's Special Relativity. In Minkowski space, the "distance" between two events is measured using the spacetime interval:

ds² = −dt² + dx²

Unlike Euclidean geometry, this metric has a minus sign in front of the time component, giving rise to three types of separations:

  • Timelike (ds² < 0): Events that can be causally connected; a massive particle can travel between them.
  • Spacelike (ds² > 0): Events that are too far apart in space for any signal to connect them.
  • Lightlike (ds² = 0): Events connected by a light ray, lying on the light cone.

Zoomed In: See the infinitesimal displacement ds as a right triangle with legs dt and dx, alongside the metric tensor η.
Zoomed Out: Drag the two events P₁ and P₂ around the grid to see whether their separation is timelike, spacelike, or lightlike. The light cone stays fixed at the origin as a reference.
