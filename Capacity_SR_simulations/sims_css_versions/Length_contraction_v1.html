ℹ About the Simulation

This interactive simulation demonstrates length contraction, a fundamental prediction of Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity (1905).

When an object moves at a significant fraction of the speed of light (c) relative to an observer, the observer measures the object's length along the direction of motion to be shorter than its proper length (rest length).

L = L₀ / γ = L₀ √(1 − β²)

The Planet frame shows the planet at rest as a sphere of proper diameter D₀, while the rocket moves and its length along the direction of motion contracts. The Rocket frame shows the rocket at rest with proper length L₀, while the planet moves in the opposite direction — its diameter along motion contracts as D∥ = D₀√(1 − β²), while the diameter perpendicular to motion stays D₀.

Both perspectives are equally valid — this is the principle of relativity. Use the toggle to switch between frames and the sliders to adjust parameters.

On-screen motion (rocket and planet drift, star parallax) is schematic: speed scales with β for visualization only and is not a literal integration of x = βct in pixel units. Contracted lengths and γ are computed from the formulas above.

Rocket and planet sizes on screen are scaled for layout (sliders map nonlinearly to pixels). Only contraction ratios (√(1 − β²)) are faithful to the physics, not absolute meter-to-kilometer scale between objects.
