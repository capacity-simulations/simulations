📖 About the Experiment

The 1887 Michelson-Morley experiment attempted to detect the "luminiferous ether" - a hypothetical medium for light propagation.

Light is split by a semi-silvered mirror (half-silvered plate) into two perpendicular beams, reflected back, and recombined. If ether existed, the beams would travel at different speeds, causing interference pattern shifts.

A compensating plate is placed in the horizontal arm to ensure both beams pass through the same thickness of glass, equalizing optical path lengths.

Result: No shift was detected, disproving the ether theory and paving the way for Einstein's special relativity.
