ℹ️ About the Simulation

This simulation demonstrates Einstein's famous equation E = mc², which describes the equivalence of mass and energy.

The equation states that energy (E) equals mass (m) multiplied by the speed of light (c) squared. Since c ≈ 299,792,458 m/s, even a tiny amount of mass contains an enormous amount of energy.

Click "Convert Mass" to watch mass transform into pure energy, visualized as photons radiating outward. This principle underlies nuclear reactions and explains how stars produce their immense energy.
