About the Simulation

This simulation demonstrates Galilean Transformations, which describe how positions and velocities transform between reference frames moving at constant velocity relative to each other.

A ball moves inside a train (S' frame) while the train moves along the ground (S frame). The ground observer sees the ball's velocity as the sum of the train's velocity and the ball's velocity relative to the train.

x' = x - v·t
t' = t
u = u' + v

v = train velocity  |  u' = ball velocity in S'  |  u = ball velocity in S

S - Stationary Frame (Ground)
S' - Moving Frame (Train)
