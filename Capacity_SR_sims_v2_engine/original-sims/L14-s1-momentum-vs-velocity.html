Simulation
Simulation 1 — Momentum vs velocity (guided inquiry)
Concept
Relativistic momentum p = γm₀v grows linearly at low speed but diverges as v → c, unlike the classical p = m₀v.
Serves
Distinguish rest mass from relativistic energy–momentum; appreciate why rest mass is invariant (m₀ stays fixed, γ drives the divergence); construct the four-momentum (p is its spatial component).
Inquiry
Expected behaviour near v = 0? Near v = c? Effect of doubling the rest mass m₀?
Misconception
Encoded in the prediction choices: (i) momentum approaches a finite maximum at v = c; (ii) doubling m₀ leaves the curve unchanged.

What stays fixed?

▾
Rest mass m₀ is Lorentz-invariant — the same number in every inertial frame. It does not change with v; the slider just lets you compare different particles.

What actually runs away is γ = 1/√(1 − v²/c²), the geometric factor multiplying m₀v. So the divergence comes from γ, never from any change in mass. In the four-momentum pμ = (E/c, p⃗), the spatial part is exactly p⃗ = γm₀v⃗.
