Concept
The spacetime interval between two events determines whether their time ordering is frame-invariant (timelike) or frame-dependent (spacelike).
Serves
Analyse causality using lightcone structure · Explain why the speed of light is an absolute speed limit · Connect superluminal propagation, acausality and instabilities.
Inquiry
Where does E₂ fall relative to E₁'s lightcone? Can a boost reverse the time ordering? What rule connects the classification to reversibility?
Misconception
"Time ordering is absolute" — that if E₁ precedes E₂ in one frame it must in every frame.

The rule (notes)

▾

Timelike (s² > 0, inside the lightcone): a causal signal could connect E₁ and E₂. The causal order is frame-invariant — no subluminal boost reverses it.

Spacelike (s² < 0, outside the lightcone): the spatial gap exceeds c × the time gap, so no signal can connect them. "Before" and "after" are frame-dependent — a boost at v = Δt/Δx flips the order.

Lightlike (s² = 0, on the cone): only a light signal connects them; the order is invariant (flipping would need v = c).
