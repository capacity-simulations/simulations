About: Spacetime Diagram Explorer

Source

Lecture
Lecture 5 — Spacetime (events, frames, spacetime diagrams, measuring distances and time).
Simulation
Simulation 2 — Spacetime diagram explorer (guided-inquiry).
Concept
Events are located by coordinates (ct, x) on a spacetime diagram; pairs of events are characterised by their separations cΔt and Δx, which reveal simultaneity (Δt = 0) or co-location (Δx = 0).
Serves
Define events & reference frames; construct/interpret spacetime diagrams; represent worldlines of particles and observers; appreciate the physical meaning of simultaneity.
Inquiry
Make co-location appear (Δx = 0); draw a worldline and read its velocity vs the 45° light line; can two events be both simultaneous and co-located?
Misconception
Confusing the axes — believing that same-x events are simultaneous. Targeted by the "Co-located" prediction choice and its rebuttal.

Investigate

▾
1.Click E₁ then E₃ to select them — they share x = −1, so they're already co-located (Δx = 0) and the connector runs vertically. Now drag E₃ sideways and watch Δx and the connector change.
2.Turn on ✏ Add worldline and click two points to draw one. Read the velocity it reports. What happens to its angle and to v as the line approaches the 45° light line?
3.Can you arrange two events that are simultaneous AND co-located at the same time? What would that mean physically?

How to use

▾
  • Add event: click an empty spot.
  • Move event: drag a circle (snaps to ½ light-second).
  • Compare a pair: click two events — the readout below updates.
  • Worldline: press ✏ Add worldline, then click two points.
  • Delete: right-click (or long-press) an event/worldline.
  • Detail 1·2·3: overlays projections & the separation triangle.
Event (a point in spacetime)
ct — time coordinate × c; x — space coordinate. Both in light-seconds (c = 1), frame S.
Separations of a pair
cΔt — difference in time coordinates × c; Δx — difference in space coordinates. Signed (order = first→second selected).
Simultaneous in S
Same horizontal grid line → same time.
Co-located in S
Same vertical grid line → same place.
Worldline velocity (slope)
Vertical ⇒ v = 0 (at rest); 45° ⇒ |v| = c (light); tilted past 45° ⇒ |v| > c (flagged ⚠).
Light lines
The dashed 45° diagonals through the origin (c = 1, so 45° on this grid).
