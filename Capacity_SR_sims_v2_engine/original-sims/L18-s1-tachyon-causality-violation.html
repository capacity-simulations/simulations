Concept
A faster-than-light signal between two spacelike-separated events can have its time-ordering reversed in a boosted frame, enabling a round-trip causal loop that violates causality.
Serves
Explain why the speed of light is an absolute speed limit · Analyse causality using lightcone structure · Connect superluminal propagation, acausality and instabilities.
Inquiry
Using spacetime intervals and frame dependence: can a signal travelling faster than light ever create a causal paradox? Build a scenario that demonstrates this or rules it out.
Misconception
"Faster-than-light signals can still respect causality as long as they aren't infinitely fast" — probed by prediction option B with a targeted rebuttal.

Lorentz boost (display in frame S′)

Every event, worldline and light cone is re-computed under this boost — not just relabelled.

Lorentz factor

β is the velocity of frame S′ relative to S, in units of c.

Spacetime interval (invariant)

>0 spacelike · <0 timelike · =0 lightlike. Identical in every frame.

Time-order reversal

Δt′ flips sign when v_boost > c²Δt/Δx — only possible for spacelike pairs (|Δx| > c|Δt|).

Return-signal arrival

x₂ = E₂'s position, sent back toward x = 0. Causal loop when ct₃ < ct₁. E₁ & E₃ both lie on x = 0, so this ordering is frame-invariant.
