About: Lorentz Boost vs Euclidean Rotation

Source

Lecture
10 — Lorentz transformations (Minkowski metric · inertial frames · Lorentz transformations).
Simulation
Simulation 2 — Lorentz transformation vs Euclidean rotation (guided inquiry).
Concept
A Lorentz boost is a hyperbolic rotation in spacetime that preserves the invariant hyperbola (ct)²−x²=1, just as an ordinary rotation preserves the circle x²+y²=1 — but the two transformations differ in how their axes move.
Serves
Interpret Lorentz transformations as hyperbolic rotations; identify invariant quantities under Lorentz transformations; describe Minkowski spacetime and its metric structure.
Inquiry
What angle do the rotated axes keep? Do the boosted axes stay perpendicular? Which quantities stay fixed as θ and β change?
Misconception
"A Lorentz boost is just a rotation, so the primed axes stay perpendicular." (Encoded as prediction choice A.)

Read the symbols

▾
  • θ = rotation angle (left panel), 0°–80°.
  • β = v/c, the boost speed (right panel), 0–0.80.
  • γ = 1/√(1−β²), the Lorentz factor.
  • black axes = unprimed frame (x, y / x, ct).
  • orange axes = primed frame (x′, y′ / x′, ct′).
  • teal curve = the invariant (circle / hyperbolas).
  • dashed = the light cone ct = ±x.

The faint purple ring sweeps each curve — its invariant stays 1.00 the whole way. The circle uses cos/sin; the hyperbola uses cosh/sinh: that is why one is a "circular" and the other a "hyperbolic" rotation.

Euclidean rotation

Both axes rotate the same way by θ → they stay perpendicular. The preserved set is the circle.

Lorentz boost (c = 1)

ct′ tilts toward x and x′ tilts toward ct, each by arctan β → they scissor. The preserved set is a pair of hyperbolas.

How the axes differ & the invariant interval

Signature (+, −): ds² = (c dt)² − dx². Unit ticks sit where the invariant curves meet each primed axis: the ct′-axis intersects the timelike hyperbola (ct)²−x² = 1, while the x′-axis intersects the spacelike hyperbola x²−(ct)² = 1. Both curves are drawn in teal. On the left, circle ticks are evenly spaced. On the right, hyperbola ticks spread apart because the Minkowski metric is not Euclidean.
