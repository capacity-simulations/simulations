Simulation 1 — Relativity of simultaneity on a Minkowski diagram (guided-inquiry)
Concept
Two events simultaneous in one inertial frame (same ct) are generally not simultaneous in a different inertial frame — shown by the tilt of the primed (S′) coordinate axes.
Serves
Lightcone structure (axes tilt toward the 45° light line); the relativity of simultaneity (core); reading causal structure off a spacetime diagram; spacelike separation as what permits frame-dependent time-ordering.
Inquiry
How does Δct′ depend on Δx and v/c? Can Δct′ change sign (B before A)? When does Δct′ vanish?
Misconception
"They only look non-simultaneous in S′ because light from A and B takes different times to reach the observer — correct for travel time and they're simultaneous again."

Drag B toward A (shrink Δx). What happens to Δct′? Is there a separation where Δct′ vanishes?

Set v/c to 0. What do the S′ axes look like? Now raise it slowly — watch the x′ axis rotate up toward the 45° light line.

Can you make B happen before A in S′ (Δct′ > 0)? Try a negative v/c.

What stays the same

▾

The spacetime interval Δs² = Δct² − Δx² between A and B is the same in every frame. Here Δct = 0, so Δs² = −Δx² < 0 — a spacelike separation (flatter than the 45° light line). That invariant fact is precisely what permits the time‑order of A and B to differ between frames. Two timelike‑separated events would keep their order in all frames.
