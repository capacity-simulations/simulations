Lecture
12 — Twin paradox
Simulation
Simulation 1 — Twin paradox worldline comparison (guided inquiry)
Concept
The twin on the straight (inertial) worldline between two events always accumulates the most proper time — no single inertial frame keeps the travelling twin at rest for the whole journey.
Serves
Analyse the twin paradox with spacetime diagrams; distinguish inertial vs non-inertial motion; explain the role of acceleration without making it the direct cause; resolve apparent paradoxes via proper time.
Inquiry
Step 1 — predict who ages more. Step 2 — read the proper times and compare across frames. Step 3 — find whether any single inertial frame keeps B at rest, and what physical event breaks the symmetry.
Misconception
"It's symmetric — from B's view A travels away and comes back, so they age the same." (Encoded as prediction choice (c) and the Step-3 friend persona.)
