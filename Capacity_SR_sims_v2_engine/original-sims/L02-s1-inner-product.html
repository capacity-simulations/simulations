About: Inner Product Invariance Under Coordinate Rotation

Source

Lecture
Lecture 2 — Vectors (Euclidean space, metric, inner product, the velocity vector).
Simulation
Simulation 1 — lecture visual aid: vectors A and B shown in two panels whose axes differ by a rotation θ; each panel shows the components and the inner product A·B; a slider sets θ; tips are draggable.
Concept
The inner product of two vectors — and therefore each vector's norm — is invariant under rotation of the coordinate axes, even though the individual components change.
Serves
Euclidean space as a geometric structure equipped with a metric; the Kronecker delta δij as the Cartesian metric; computing inner products and norms; the velocity vector as a coordinate-independent geometric object.
Inquiry
Rotate the frame — which of these changes: the components Ax, Ay; the length |A|; or the inner product A·B? Commit a prediction before running.
Misconception
That rotating the coordinate axes changes the inner product or a vector's length (confusing component change with geometric change).

Why it is invariant — the cross terms cancel exactly

The inner product is the contraction of the components with the metric δij. In Cartesian coordinates δij is the same in every rotated frame, so although the components Ai transform, the scalar δijAiBj does not — that is precisely the equality of the two boxed values.
