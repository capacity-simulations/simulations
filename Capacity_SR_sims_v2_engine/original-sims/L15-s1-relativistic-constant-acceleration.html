About: Relativistic Constant Acceleration

Source

Lecture
15 — Relativistic dynamics (four-acceleration, four-force, equations of motion).
Simulation
Relativistic constant acceleration — guided-inquiry mode.
Concept
Under a constant applied force a relativistic particle's velocity asymptotically approaches c rather than growing without bound, while its energy increases without limit.
Serves
Formulate Newton's 2nd law in Lorentz-invariant form (F = dp/dt, p = γmv); relate relativistic force to changes in four-momentum; see how the geometric speed limit c emerges dynamically from the equation of motion.
Inquiry
Before viewing the graph: what do you expect the v-vs-t graph to look like after a long time?
Misconception
"A constant force means constant acceleration, so velocity keeps increasing forever — there's no speed limit in F = ma." Encoded as prediction choice (A) and as the diverging dashed Newtonian curve.

Inquiry · read the symbols

▾

As you watch the traces build, name what each symbol means:

  • F — applied force (lab frame), set by the slider.
  • a₀ = F/m — proper acceleration (constant, frame-invariant); set by the slider.
  • v — velocity; c — speed of light (set to 1, so the axis is v/c).
  • p = γmv — relativistic momentum; γ = 1/√(1−v²/c²) — Lorentz factor.
  • E = γmc² — total energy; m — rest mass = 1, at rest initially.
① The force is the same in both predictions — so where does the "missing" velocity (the shaded gap up to the dashed Newtonian line) go? Look at the energy plot for the answer.
② Even though v levels off just below c, the energy keeps climbing — the work done by the constant force pours into γ (momentum & energy), not into more speed.

Same force, opposite fates

▾
  • velocity v → c — bounded, asymptotic, never reached.
  • energy E → ∞ — unbounded, grows forever.

The speed limit isn't a rule added by hand. It emerges from F = dp/dt once momentum is the relativistic p = γmv: as v → c, γ blows up, so additional force buys ever less speed but ever more energy.

Newton's 2nd law in Lorentz-invariant form: force = rate of change of relativistic momentum.
Lorentz factor — diverges as v → c, which is what stops the speed-up.
Velocity for constant F starting from rest — strictly bounded by c (solid curve).
Total energy = γmc² — grows without bound (lower plot).
Newtonian prediction (dashed grey) — an unbounded straight line.
Long-time limits: speed saturates at c, energy diverges. That is the whole insight.
