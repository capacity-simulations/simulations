About: Photon Worldline & Vanishing Proper Time

17 — Photon
Simulation
Simulation 1 (guided inquiry): the photon worldline on a Minkowski diagram.
Concept
A photon travels along a lightlike worldline (45°) whose spacetime interval is exactly zero, so zero proper time elapses for the photon — a limit massive particles approach but never reach as β → 1.
Serves
Massless particle; proper time; representing photon worldlines in spacetime diagrams; explaining why proper time vanishes for photons.
Inquiry
Predict the photon's Δs² and Δτ between E₁ and E₂; predict what happens to a massive particle's proper time as you push β toward 1.
Misconception
"If I travel fast enough I can catch up with a photon and see it at rest" — addressed structurally (slider cannot reach β = 1, prediction option (d), and the friend persona prompt).

A friend says…

▾
"If I travel fast enough I can catch up with a photon and see it at rest — just like running alongside a train."

How would you respond? Explore the slider first, then reveal one good answer.

No matter how close β gets to 1, the photon's worldline stays at exactly 45° in every inertial frame — light always travels at c. The slider shows it: the massive worldline approaches 45° but never matches it, and E₂′ never quite reaches E₂. There is no inertial frame in which a photon is at rest, so you can never ride alongside one. The invariance of c is a postulate of special relativity, and Δτ → 0 confirms that "catching" light would demand zero proper time — the photon's own clock never advances.
