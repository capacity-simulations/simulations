9 — Proper Time
Simulation
Simulation 1 (guided inquiry): Worldline length and proper time
Concept
The straight (inertial) worldline between two events maximizes the proper time elapsed — opposite to Euclidean intuition about the "shortest path."
Serves
Define proper time as an invariant along a worldline; explain the geometric meaning of elapsed time in spacetime; relate proper time to physical clock measurements.
Inquiry
Predict which of three reunited travellers ages most; construct each worldline and record proper time; rank them; optionally switch to S′ and check whether τ (vs Δt′) changes.
Misconception
"The traveller who took the longer path through space must have aged more — they covered more distance." Encoded as prediction option (a); rebuttal appears only when selected.

Proper time of a timelike segment

Δτ ≡ proper time = the time read by a clock carried along the segment. Natural units, c = 1.

Spacetime interval — signature (+,−)

ds² > 0 ⇒ timelike ⇒ real proper time. The minus sign on Δx is why detours shorten τ.

Total proper time along a worldline

The inertial (straight) worldline is the unique path that maximizes this sum.

Lorentz boost to S′  (β ≡ v/c, γ ≡ 1/√(1−β²))

Coordinate time Δt′ is frame-dependent; proper time τ is invariant — equal in S and S′.
