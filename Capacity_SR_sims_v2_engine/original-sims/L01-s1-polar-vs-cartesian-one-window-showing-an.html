Concept
Coordinates are labels we choose to describe the same physical motion — not the motion itself. Identical circular motion gives sinusoidal Cartesian traces but trivially constant/linear polar traces.
Serves
Use Cartesian and polar coordinates to describe points and trajectories; understand coordinates as labels rather than physical observables; geometry as a mathematical description of space.
Structural target
The intuition that complicated coordinate behaviour implies complicated motion — refuted because the same circle is two sinusoids in Cartesian yet r = const, θ = linear in polar.
