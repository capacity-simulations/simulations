16 — E = mc²
Simulation
Simulation 1 — Energy–momentum conservation in a collision (guided inquiry)
Concept
In relativistic collisions total four-momentum is conserved, so kinetic energy can convert into rest mass — the rest mass of products is generally not the sum of the incoming rest masses.
Serves
Derive the relativistic energy–momentum relation; explain rest energy; interpret mass–energy equivalence; apply energy–momentum conservation in relativistic processes.
Inquiry
Two identical particles of rest mass m collide head-on at equal & opposite β = 0.8 and merge. Predict the rest mass of the combined particle — 2m, more, or less?
Misconception
That rest masses add like ordinary masses (M = m₁ + m₂), ignoring the kinetic-energy contribution.
