About: Dodgeball — Galilean Velocity Addition

Source

Lecture
6 — Galilean Relativity
Simulation
Simulation 1 — Dodgeball (guided inquiry)
Concept
The velocity of an object depends on the observer's reference frame; Galilean velocity addition v′ = v − Vframe transforms velocities between any two inertial frames, with none being privileged.
Serves
Elements: relative motion, relative speed, relative velocity. Outcomes: describe relative motion between inertial observers; apply Galilean transformations to positions and velocities; explain the principle of Galilean relativity.
Inquiry
Requiring students to select a frame before reading velocities makes frame-dependence unavoidable by design; the adjustable velocities of A and B let students explore Galilean velocity addition.
Misconception
"The ground frame is the natural one to use — its velocities are the real ones." Surfaced only via prediction option (c) and the friend-persona prompt.

Guided steps

▾

Use the Step ◂ ▸ buttons (top bar) to walk through. All controls stay live at every step.

  1. Step 1 — Observe A's rest frame. A is at rest; B and the ball move. Read each velocity in the strip below the field. Note the ball's speed before you switch.
  2. Step 2 — Click a different frame (try B's). B stops, the court changes its scroll rate, and every velocity updates at once. Compare the ball's speed before vs after.
  3. Step 3 — Try the ball's rest frame. The ball stops; A and B move. The ball-reaches-B event still happens at the same instant — the physics is identical.
  4. Step 4 — Reflect. Read your friend's claim under "Think about it" and use what you saw to answer.

Think about it

▾

A friend says: "The ground frame is the natural one to use — the velocities in that frame are the real ones."

After exploring all three frames, what would you tell them? Look for evidence in the sim: the court markings move in every frame you pick (the ground is at rest in none of them, except when the selected frame's ground velocity happens to be zero — e.g. vA = 0 in A's frame, vB = 0 in B's frame), and the ball reaches B at the same instant no matter which frame measures it.
