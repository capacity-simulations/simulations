Lecture
8 — Lightcone (Simultaneity · Relative time · Rotations in spacetime)
Simulation
Simulation 2 — Spacetime diagram explorer (single frame), mode: guided-inquiry
Concept
The spacetime interval Δs² = (cΔt)² − (Δx)² classifies an event pair as timelike, lightlike, or spacelike — a frame-invariant geometric fact — and an event's coordinate time is distinct from when its light reaches the observer.
Serves
Define the lightcone structure; distinguish timelike / spacelike / null separations; interpret causal structure with spacetime diagrams; separate coordinate time from light-reception time as groundwork for the relativity of simultaneity.
Inquiry
Which events lie inside E₁'s future light cone? How does the sign of Δs² relate to inside / on / outside the cone? Are an event's coordinate time and reception time ever equal — and what sets the gap?
Misconception
"Simultaneous events are seen together; light-travel delay is just an illusion you can subtract away." Encoded as prediction option (A); the targeted rebuttal appears only on selection.
Review note
The S′ boost slider was removed per expert review: with no second-frame axes it was visually dishonest and ambiguously pinned the observer. This is a clean single-frame explorer.
