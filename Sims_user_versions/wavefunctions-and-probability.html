📊 About This Simulation

Physics: The particle is prepared in a Gaussian wavefunction ψ(x). Measurement outcomes follow the Born rule, so the position probability density is |ψ(x)|². Repeated measurements on identically prepared states reproduce the theoretical distribution.

Simulation visuals: The top plot shows the theoretical wavefunction view (probability, real part, imaginary part, or both real/imaginary). The bottom plot shows measured outcomes as a normalized histogram, overlaid with the theoretical |ψ(x)|² curve for comparison.

Controls: Run single-shot or batch measurements, enable auto-measurement and set its rate, adjust animation speed, switch view mode, and reset measurements. The Statistics panel reports total count, mean position, measured standard deviation, theoretical σ, and convergence.
