About this simulation

Physics: The Stern-Gerlach experiment measures spin along a chosen axis. Z-magnets split atoms into Z+ and Z−, while X-magnets split into X+ and X−. Switching measurement axis changes the state basis, so prior spin information along a different axis is no longer definite.

Visuals: The 3D view shows the atom source, magnet stages, optional blockers, beam branching, and detector hits. Beam direction and color encode the current spin channel, and the legend maps colors to unpolarized, Z, and X outcomes.

Controls: Build Stage 2/3 by dragging magnets or blockers, set Stage 1 orientation/blocker, tune emission rate and field strength, and use play/pause, reset, and stats controls. Turn on Explore to see hover explanations on apparatus parts.
