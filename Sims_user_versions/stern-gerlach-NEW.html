Stern-Gerlach Experiment

About the Simulation

Physics: In an inhomogeneous magnetic field, atoms feel a spin-dependent force along z. Quantum mechanics predicts discrete outcomes: for total spin \(j\), the beam splits into \(2j+1\) channels labeled by \(m_j=-j,\ldots,+j\).

Visuals: The left canvas shows source, magnet, trajectories, and impact spots on the detector window. In quantum mode you see separated bands by \(m_j\); in classical mode you see a continuous spread.

Controls: Set Total Spin \(j\), Magnetic Field Gradient \(\partial B/\partial z\), and Beam Intensity. Use Switch to Classical Prediction to compare models, Reset to clear counts, and Hover & Explore for inline hints.

$$F_z = -g_J \, m_J \, \mu_B \, \frac{\partial B}{\partial z}$$
