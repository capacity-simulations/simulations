About This Simulation

Physics. Spherical harmonics Yℓm(θ, φ) are the angular eigenfunctions of orbital angular momentum in quantum mechanics. The integer ℓ sets the overall nodal complexity, while m sets the azimuthal structure around the z-axis.

Axes. The white z-axis (vertical) is the polar/quantization axis from which θ is measured. This matches the standard QM convention where Lz eigenstates have definite m.

Visuals. In Polar Surface, the radius is proportional to |Y|. In Projection, the surface is a fixed sphere colored by Y (real: sign + magnitude emphasis; complex: hue and brightness from phase and |Y|). In Point Cloud, angular acceptance is proportional to |Y|² while radius is mapped as r ∝ |Y|. Color encodes the field: Real uses a red↔blue sign/magnitude scale, while Complex uses hue for phase arg(Y) and brightness for |Y|.

Controls: Left-drag rotate · Scroll/pinch zoom · Right-drag (or two-finger drag) pan
