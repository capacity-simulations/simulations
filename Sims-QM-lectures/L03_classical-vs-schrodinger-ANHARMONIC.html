Quantum vs Classical Dynamics

Physics

Classical Newtonian motion in the anharmonic well V(x) = ½mω²x² + εx⁴ is compared with quantum time evolution from the Schrödinger equation using a split-operator method. The classical particle and the Gaussian wave packet start at the same initial displacement; anharmonicity makes the classical period amplitude-dependent and causes the quantum packet to spread and interfere differently than in a pure harmonic oscillator.

Simulation visuals

The plot shows the potential V(x), a classical particle (dot) moving along x, the probability density |ψ(x)|² (filled curve), and horizontal lines for classical total energy and quantum expectation value ⟨E⟩q so you can compare energy consistency over time.

Controls

Use Reset and Play/Pause in the header to restart or run time evolution. The speed slider scales wall-clock integration rate. Sliders adjust mass m, angular frequency ω, initial amplitude A, and quartic strength ε. Statistics and numerical diagnostics update live; the Legend explains each graphic element.
