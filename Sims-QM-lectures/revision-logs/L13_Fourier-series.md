EC 03/30/26: Added a "Gaussian in a box" as a third wave-type option alongside the square and sawtooth waves, using the analytically known particle-in-a-box sin-only basis coefficients (b_n ∝ sin(nπ/2) exp(−n²π²σ²/2L²)) with an adjustable σ/L slider, domain switching to [0, L], and updated axis labels, equation bar, tooltip, and narration. This lets students contrast the super-exponential convergence of a smooth Gaussian with the 1/n Gibbs-phenomenon regime of the discontinuous waves, directly illustrating how regularity of the target function controls the rate of Fourier convergence.
EC 04/03/26: Added a fourth "Two Gaussians" mode using the same sine basis on [0, L], with analytically combined coefficients b_n ∝ [sin(nπ/4) + sin(nπ/2)] exp(−n²π²σ²/2L²) for peaks centered at L/4 and L/2. The wave-type selector was reorganized into four separate buttons, the tooltip/narration/equation bar were updated to explain the new mode and its activation of even-n harmonics, and a lecture-mode-display checkbox was added to hide the control-panel heading, simulation-context narration panel, and the square/sawtooth options for a cleaner classroom-facing view. Follow-up UI fixes also made the small Fourier-component plots redraw crisply during panel resizing, reduced the default equation font size, and constrained the two-Gaussian width control to a narrower pedagogical range (max σ/L = 0.15, default σ/L = 0.05).
