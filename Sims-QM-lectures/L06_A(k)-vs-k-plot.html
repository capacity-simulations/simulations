A(k) vs k Plot

About This Simulation

This interactive simulation visualizes the Gaussian momentum amplitude distribution A(k) commonly encountered in quantum mechanics when describing wavepackets. The amplitude function governs how momentum components are distributed around a central wavenumber k₀.

Students can drag k₀ to shift the peak (changing the particle's central momentum) and adjust σ to widen or narrow the distribution. Shaded regions show the amplitude A(k) over ±σ and ±2σ ranges. The percentages 68.27% and 95.45% refer to the fraction of ∫|A(k)|² dk (probability in k-space) within those ranges; most of the wavepacket's momentum content clusters near k₀. Click or drag on the plot to move the marker and read A(k) at that k.

where k₀ is the central wavenumber and σ characterizes the width of the distribution in k-space.
