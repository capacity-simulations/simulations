About the simulation
About this simulation

This simulation compares a classical harmonic oscillator with a quantum coherent-state wavepacket in the same harmonic potential V(x) = 1/2 mω²x². The left column follows Newton's equation of motion; the right column follows the Schrödinger equation for the same m, ω, and amplitude A.

Use the shared sliders to change m, ω, and A. The coherent-state center satisfies ⟨x⟩(t) = A cos(ωt), so it follows the same trajectory as the classical particle. The nonzero tails beyond ±A show probability in a classically forbidden region, not escape from the trap.
