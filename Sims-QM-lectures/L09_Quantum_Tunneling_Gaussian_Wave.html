About this simulation

This simulation shows a quantum-mechanical Gaussian wavepacket incident on a potential barrier. Unlike plane waves, wavepackets are localized in space and spread over time.

The packet is initialized with a localized Gaussian envelope and propagates toward a symmetric rectangular barrier with V(x) = U for |x| < a. Upon interaction, part of the packet reflects and part tunnels through (if E < U) or transmits above the barrier (if E > U). The model is non-relativistic, one-dimensional, spinless quantum mechanics for a particle with the free electron mass.

The spatial grid uses an FFT, so the domain is technically periodic; absorbing layers at the far left and right approximate an open system and reduce spurious reflections. The reflection/transmission readout is an estimate built from separated probability plus absorbed weight, so it is useful for visual intuition but is not an exact flux coefficient.

The regime badge (tunneling / above / threshold) compares carrier E to U. For mean initial energy, compare ⟨H⟩₀ (Wavepacket Info) to U—narrow packets can have ⟨H⟩₀ > U while carrier E < U.
