About the Simulation Physics: A 2D Gaussian wavepacket evolves under free-particle spreading (ℏ = m = 1). The probability density |ψ(x,y,t)|² is normalized on the full plane, so ∫∫|ψ|² dx dy = 1 at all times. Pressing Measure performs a position measurement: the outcome is sampled exactly from |ψ|² within the finite viewing window (renormalized there by the Born rule), and the state collapses to a narrow Gaussian centered at the measured point, approximating a position eigenstate. The post-collapse packet then re-spreads according to the uncertainty principle.

Visuals: In 3D mode, surface height is proportional to |ψ|² (clamped when the peak exceeds the display range; the actual peak is shown in the statistics panel). In Heatmap mode, color maps to |ψ|² on a fixed scale with a legend. The plotted square is finite, so the visible probability can be slightly below 1 when tails extend beyond the view.

Controls: Use Play/Reset, switch between 3D and Heatmap views, and press Measure to trigger collapse. Sliders set initial position, width, and animation speed. All quantities are in natural units (ℏ = m = 1).
