About the Simulation

Visualizing the high-energy harmonic oscillator in natural units ℏ = m = ω = 1.

As n → ∞, ΔE/Eₙ → 0: the spacing between nearby energy levels becomes small compared with the total energy.

Key physics: The probability density |ψ_n(x)|² is time-independent for an energy eigenstate. The wavefunction panel shows the phase rotation through Re(ψ) and Im(ψ), while the probability panel compares |ψ_n(x)|² with the classical distribution.

The orange classical curve diverges at the turning points, so the display clips only the visible height near those endpoints. The shaded region is there to make the classical support easier to compare with the quantum probability density.
