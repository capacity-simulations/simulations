About the Simulation
Physics: This simulation follows the Lecture 6 construction: a free-particle wavefunction is built from A(k), evolved with the lecture's α(t), and displayed as Re[\sqrt{C}\psi], Im[\sqrt{C}\psi], and P(x,t).

Visualization: The upper plot shows the real and imaginary parts of \sqrt{C}\psi(x,t). The lower plot shows the lecture probability density P(x,t) = C|\psi|^2. For each slider choice, the vertical axes are fixed from the initial state rather than changing during the time evolution.

Controls: Adjust k₀, σ, and m. Here σ is the lecture's momentum-space width, so smaller σ produces a broader packet in position space.
