About the Simulation

The double-slit experiment demonstrates wave-particle duality. When particles pass through two slits, they create an interference pattern on the detection screen.

Quantum (green): |ψ₁ + ψ₂|² — amplitudes add, then square. The interference fringes arise from constructive/destructive interference.

Classical (orange): P₁ + P₂ — a broad spray of bullets hits the barrier, only bullets passing through slit 1 or slit 2 continue, and the screen shows the direct sum of the two smooth one-slit bullet distributions.

The key difference: quantum adds amplitudes (producing fringes), classical bullets add probabilities (no fringes) — Feynman Lectures Vol III, Ch 1.
