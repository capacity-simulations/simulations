About the Simulation

The double-slit experiment demonstrates wave-particle duality. When particles pass through two slits, they create an interference pattern on the detection screen.

Quantum (green): |ψ₁ + ψ₂|² — amplitudes add, then square. The interference fringes arise from constructive/destructive interference.

Classical (orange): A broad spray of particles is incident on the slits, and pass through either slit 1 or slit 2.

Close a slit: Use the S1/S2 buttons to close one slit and see single-slit diffraction. Reduce λ into the UV range to see sinc² zeros and secondary maxima. Notice that some screen positions receive fewer particles with two slits open than with one — evidence of destructive interference!
