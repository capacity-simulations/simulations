About this simulation

Physics

For a 1D symmetric rectangular barrier V(x) = U for −a < x < a (full width 2a, height U), the plane-wave transmission probability T(E) depends on mass m and energy E. Below the barrier (E < U), transmission occurs via quantum tunnelling; above (E > U), T oscillates with barrier width. Conservation of probability gives R + T = 1, where R is the reflection probability.

Formulas

E < U (tunneling — derived in Lecture 9):

\[ T = \frac{4k^2\eta^2}{4k^2\eta^2 + (k^2+\eta^2)^2\sinh^2(2\eta a)} \]

E > U (above barrier — obtained by substituting η → iκ):

\[ T = \frac{4k^2\kappa^2}{4k^2\kappa^2 + (k^2-\kappa^2)^2\sin^2(2\kappa a)} \]
\[ \eta = \sqrt{\frac{2m(U-E)}{\hbar^2}}\,,\quad \kappa = \sqrt{\frac{2m(E-U)}{\hbar^2}}\,,\quad E = \frac{\hbar^2 k^2}{2m} \]

The simulation sets ℏ = 1 in the computation. The E > U formula extends the lecture derivation, which covers the tunneling case explicitly.

Simulation visuals

The main plot shows T (and optionally R = 1 − T) vs energy or wavenumber; hover for readouts. A moving marker scans the curve (optional). The barrier diagram below the plot is a schematic visualization of the incident, transmitted, and barrier-interior waves.

Controls

View mode: T vs E or T vs k. Parameters: half-width a, barrier height U, mass m, scanner speed. Display: classical prediction, region shading, resonance markers (T = 1), animated scanner. Top bar: Reset, Start/Pause (animation). Theme: dark/light toggle. Current values panel shows T, E, k, and η or related quantity for the active point.
