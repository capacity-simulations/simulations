Classical Particle on a Harmonic Hill

Equation
\( m\ddot{x} = -\dfrac{dV}{dx} \)
\( V(x) = \tfrac12 kx^2 \)
\( \ddot{x} = -\dfrac{k}{m}x \)
Controls
Mass m 1.0
Spring Constant k 1.0
Notes
A single classical particle moves in one dimension in the harmonic potential \( V(x) = \tfrac12 kx^2 \). Changing \( m \) or \( k \) changes the acceleration \( \ddot{x} = -(k/m)x \).
