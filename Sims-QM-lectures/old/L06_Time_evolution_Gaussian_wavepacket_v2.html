ℹ About the Simulation
Physics: A Gaussian wavepacket is a quantum state localized in both position and momentum. Unlike classical particles, it spreads over time due to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle — different momentum components travel at different speeds, causing dispersion. The packet also propagates through space at the group velocity, determined by its initial momentum.

Visualization: The upper plot displays the wavefunction's real (blue) and imaginary (purple) components, showing the oscillatory nature of quantum waves. The lower plot shows the probability density |ψ|² — the likelihood of finding the particle at each position.

Controls: Adjust x₀ to set the starting position, k₀ to control initial momentum and propagation direction, σ to change the initial width (smaller = faster spreading), and m to vary the particle mass (heavier = slower spreading). Use the Display toggles to show/hide wavefunction components.
