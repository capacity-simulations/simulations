About the simulation
About this simulation

This simulation compares a classical harmonic oscillator (mass on a spring) with a quantum coherent-state wavepacket in the same parabolic potential V(x) = ½kx². The four panels show the harmonic potential, the classical mass-spring system, the potential with classically forbidden regions, and the quantum probability density |ψ(x,t)|².

Use the sliders to change mass m, stiffness k, and energy E. The quantum wavepacket center follows the classical trajectory exactly — this is Ehrenfest's theorem. Drag to rotate and scroll to zoom where applicable; use the Simulation Context panel for narrated steps.

Harmonic oscillator

The energy of the oscillator depends on amplitude A and angular frequency ω:

E = ½kA²   ,   ω = √(k/m)   ,   A = √(2E/k)
