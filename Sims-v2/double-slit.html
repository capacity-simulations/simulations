About the Simulation

The double-slit experiment demonstrates the wave-particle duality of quantum mechanics. When waves pass through two narrow slits, they create an interference pattern on the detection screen.

Plane wavefronts approach the slits from a source, split into two secondary circular waves (S1 and S2), and interfere constructively and destructively to create the characteristic pattern.

Toggle the Classical Expectation button to overlay what classical physics would predict (two peaks behind each slit) versus the actual quantum interference pattern.
