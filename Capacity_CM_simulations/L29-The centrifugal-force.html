"The Centrifugal Force — A Ball on a Merry-Go-Round" · 3D carousel with two synchronized views (ground/inertial ↔ rotating camera, plus side-by-side), trails in both frames, counterfactual "fling" mode
Concept
The centrifugal force is not a real interaction: it appears only when motion is described in a rotating frame (Fcf = mω²r, radially outward); the ground view shows straight-line or at-rest motion under real forces only.
Serves
Frame-dependence of fictitious forces · Newton's 1st law as the ground-frame explanation · reading and scaling F = mω²r (kernel-verified: m = 1 kg, ω = 1.5 rad/s, r = 2 m → 4.5 N outward; ω = 1, r = 1 → 1 N; F ∝ ω² and ∝ r)
Inquiry
Where is the outward force in the ground view? · How does Fcf change when ω doubles? · Which facts are identical in both frames?
Misconception
"Centrifugal force is a real outward interaction acting on the ball." Nothing exerts it; the released ball's ground-frame path is a straight tangent line, never a radial fling — the outward push exists only in the rotating description.

2 · Guided inquiry

✓▾
  1. Feel the "push". Camera on Rotating, press ▶ Play. After release the ball drifts outward as if shoved by the violet arrow, Fcf = mω²r = 4.50 N at release — and the arrow grows as r grows (Fcf ∝ r).
  2. Hunt for the pusher. Switch to Ground. After release, which horizontal force arrows remain? None. The cyan trail is a straight tangent line at constant v = ωr = 3.00 m/s (gravity ↓ and the normal force ↑ cancel). Newton's 1st law is the whole story.
  3. Scale the ghost force. With r = 2.0 m, set ω = 1.00 then 2.00 rad/s and read the badge: 2.00 N → 8.00 N. Double ω ⇒ ×4 force, because Fcf = mω²r ∝ ω². (Moving a slider restarts the run.)
  4. Race the claim. Press ⚠ "Fling" claim: a red ball follows the radial-fling prediction while the white ball does the real thing. Compare where each crosses the yellow rim — from both cameras.
  5. End state. Beyond the rim the floor has friction (μ = 0.40, so a = μg = 3.92 m/s² backward) and the ball stops. Ground camera: it just sits there. Rotating camera: it appears to "orbit". Toggle the Coriolis arrow chip (rotating view) to see how a spinning observer must account for that circling.

Invariant (frame-proof): the events — release point, rim crossing, resting spot — and every real force (backstop push, gravity, normal, floor friction). Switching cameras changes the description, never the physics. That is the fingerprint of a fictitious force: it appears and disappears with your choice of frame, no object exerts it, and it has no reaction partner.

Formal tools — the same motion in two coordinate systems (m = 1 kg, SI units, g = 9.81 m/s²)
Ground frame · after release
A straight line at constant speed. The sim draws exactly this closed form — no integrator, no force.
Rotating frame = re-description
The orange path is the same straight line rotated into carousel coordinates — a viewpoint change, not new physics.
Newton in the rotating frame
To keep F = ma in a spinning frame you must add these two frame terms. No object exerts them; they have no reaction partner.
Centrifugal magnitude
Here: m = 1 kg, ω = 1.50 rad/s, r = 2.0 m ⇒ Fcf = 4.50 N, radially outward — matches the header badge and the reference value (4.5 N at ω = 1.5 rad/s, r = 2 m).
While held (rotating-frame equilibrium)
The backstop's real inward (centripetal) push balances the fictitious outward pull — why the held ball sits still on the carousel.
