About This Simulation

Physics
This simulation compares a classical particle and a quantum wavepacket evolving in an anharmonic potential V(x) = x² + εx⁴. The classical particle obeys Newton's 2nd law (F = ma), while the quantum wavepacket evolves via the time-dependent Schrödinger equation using the split-operator method. At ε = 0 (harmonic oscillator), both motions overlap perfectly. Increasing ε introduces anharmonicity, causing the quantum wavepacket to disperse and fragment due to interference — a purely quantum effect.
Visuals
Top plot: The blue curve is the potential V(x). The green curve shows the quantum probability density |ψ(x,t)|². The pink ball is the classical particle on the potential. The yellow diamond marks the quantum expectation value ⟨x⟩.
Bottom plot: Trajectory comparison over time — pink for classical position, yellow for quantum ⟨x⟩.
Controls
ε slider: Anharmonicity strength (0 = harmonic).
x₀ slider: Initial displacement of both particle and wavepacket.
Speed slider: Simulation speed multiplier.
