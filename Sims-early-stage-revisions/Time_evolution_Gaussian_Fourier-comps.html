About This Simulation

⚛ Physics

A Gaussian wave packet evolving under the free-particle Schrödinger equation, built from a superposition of plane waves with different momenta.

  • Dispersion: Each momentum component k travels at phase velocity ω/k = k, causing the packet to spread over time
  • Wave function: ψ(x,t) = Σ cₖ exp(ikx − iωt) where ω = k²
  • Spreading: Width grows as σ(t) = σ₀√(1 + t²/σ₀⁴) — narrower packets spread faster

🎨 Visuals

  • Top panel: Individual Fourier modes with rotating phasors showing phase evolution e^{−iωt}
  • Bottom panel: The coherent sum — |ψ|² (probability density), Re[ψ], Im[ψ]
  • Dashed gray: Initial |ψ₀|² for comparison
  • Gold line: Classical particle position (group velocity)

🎮 Controls

  • σ — Initial width (smaller = faster spreading)
  • x₀ — Starting position of the wave packet
  • N — Number of Fourier modes to display
  • Speed — Animation playback rate
  • Show Real/Imag — Toggle visibility of Re[ψ] and Im[ψ]
